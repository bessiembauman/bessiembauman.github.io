Yale College 2021 | B.A. Economics I’m a rising senior at Yale University, where I’m studying economics. I care deeply about addressing economic inequality, so I’ve focused on studying tax policy, welfare, and community development. I have experience in direct service, academic research, and public policy. I’m always looking for opportunities to give my time and energy to causes helping low- and moderate-income families. Recently, I have started as an intern for Prosperity Now, where I’m working on the Policy Team to contribute to research on federal policies affecting low- and moderate-income families. In April, I was recognized as a member of the 2020 Class of Truman Scholars. I have also just finished a stint as a site coordinator for “Virtual VITA,” an alternative tax preparation service developed by me and my dear friend and VITA co-coordinator, Tylar Bloch. If you’re interested in getting in touch with me, please don’t hesitate to reach out! You can send me an email or connect with me on LinkedIn.

My Background

I am a proud Kansan and Child of Deaf Adults (CODA). I was raised by two Deaf parents, and my first language is American Sign Language. In my first year of high school, my father passed away from cancer. My mother is a first-generation Cuban-American, and I take great pride in being Cuban.

My family, both immediate and extended, has faced a variety of economic hardships. From my grandmother traveling to America from Cuba to build a new life to my own mother working hard to make ends meet, I understand some of the consequences of economic instability.

Now, I am a student at Yale University. I have my family, my friends, the Leadership Enterprise for a Diverse America, the Jack Kent Cooke Foundation, and the Truman Foundation to thank for my growth over time.
